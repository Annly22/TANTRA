Engineering Auction is a live, interactive engineering decision-making competition where teams design a solution by bidding on materials, manufacturing processes, and design features using virtual currency. Teams must make logical trade-offs under budget constraints and justify their decisions in real time. The event evaluates engineering thinking, feasibility, cost optimization, and teamwork.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Open to undergraduate engineering students from recognized institutions.
  • Participants must register in teams of 3 members.
  • Intercollege teams are permitted.
  • A participant may be part of only one team.
  • Each team will receive a fixed amount of fake currency at the start of the event
  • Teams must participate in live auction rounds to bid for materials, manufacturing processes, and design features.
  • All bids placed during the auction are final and non-reversible.
  • Teams may bid only if sufficient budget is available.
  • One common engineering problem statement will be provided to all teams.
  • Teams must design their solution strictly using items acquired through the auction.
  • Solutions should be practical, feasible, and logically justified.
  • External materials or pre-prepared designs are not permitted.
  • Participants must maintain professional behavior.
  • Any misconduct or equipment damage will lead to disqualification.
  • Judges and coordinators decisions are final.
  • Inter-team collaboration during the event is prohibited.
  • No physical submission is required.
  • Teams must present and verbally justify their final design.
  • Judging will be based on engineering logic, budget management, feasibility, and clarity of explanation.
